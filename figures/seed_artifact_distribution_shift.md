# The seed-42 modal collapse and the train/test-gap inversion (companion to §21–§22, §35)

*Detailed write-up for the "investigate train-test-gap-fft" thread. Brief version: §35 of
[sft_subliminal_results.md](sft_subliminal_results.md).*

## Summary

The number-sequence SFT dataset we train on (the Camila Blank et al. release,
*Subliminal Learning is Steering Vector Distillation*, arXiv:2606.00995; HF
`agu18dec/steering_vector_distillation`, run `cat_nums_30k_seed42_qwen25_7b_v1`) was
generated by passing a single sampling seed (`seed=42`) to every one of the 30,000 vLLM
requests (audited in §22). We show, from the data itself, that this collapses the dataset
onto a low-entropy mode, and that the collapse is **positional**: it is maximal at the first
generated number and decays back to i.i.d. by ~the fifth. This single fact explains a chain
of previously confusing observations: the anomalously low `val` curve in the §31 four-curve
plot, and the train/test-gap *inversion* in the §21 data-scaling ladder
([xl_ladder_distribution_shift.png](xl_ladder_distribution_shift.png)). A clean
per-distribution loss eval of the two recoverable ladder endpoints confirms that the
inversion is a measurement artifact of evaluating on the easy modal distribution, not a
statement about generalization.

## Background

Two number-sequence distributions appear throughout the cat-SFT experiments:

- **Modal / "original" data** — the Camila Blank `raw.jsonl` baseline, sampled at
  temperature 1.0 but with `SamplingParams(seed=42)` replicated across all 30k requests
  (per-request RNG streams are therefore identical). Held-out slice: `cat_val_2000.json`.
  Source `gen_summary.json`: `model=Qwen2.5-7B-Instruct, temperature=1.0, max_tokens=200,
  seed=42`.
- **Fresh / i.i.d. data** — our regeneration (`gen_xl_cat_shard.py`): same model, same
  prompt grammar, `temperature=1.0, top_p=1.0`, but `torch.manual_seed` is set **once per
  shard** (not per request), so sampling advances continuously and is genuinely i.i.d. This
  is the bulk of the XL training rungs; held-out slice: `cat_val_fresh_2000.json`.

The two are surface-statistically near-identical (completion length 39 chars, ~9.2 numbers,
rule-pass rate 1.0, pooled digit entropy ≈ 3.28 b, gzip ratio ≈ 1.41, prompt-number copy
rate ≈ 0.06). Yet a model assigns them very different cross-entropy. The resolution is below.

## 1. The collapse is positional, and it is the seed

We measured the entropy of the *k*-th generated number across each full 30k dataset (numbers
parsed left-to-right per completion):

| position *k* | Camila (seed 42) | Our i.i.d. | deficit |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 (first number) | **6.74 b**  (top value 734 → **17.2%**) | 9.62 b (top 456 → 1.2%) | 2.88 b |
| 1 | 8.41 b | 9.64 b | 1.21 b |
| 2 | 8.61 b | 9.67 b | 1.06 b |
| 3 | 8.54 b | 9.66 b | 1.12 b |
| 4 | 9.02 b | 9.65 b | 0.63 b |
| pooled (position-blind) | 9.377 b | 9.706 b | 0.33 b |
| exact-duplicate completions | 360 / 30k (1.2%) | 37 / 30k (0.1%) | — |

Two observations.

**(i) The marginal histogram hides the artifact; the positional histogram exposes it.**
Pooling all numbers position-blind, the two datasets differ by only 0.33 b — which is why
every marginal/digit-level proxy looked identical. Conditioning on position, the first number
differs by 2.88 b: in the Camila data **a single value (734) is the first number 17.2% of the
time** (5,171 / 30,000 completions), versus a near-uniform 1.2% in the i.i.d. data.

**(ii) The deficit decays with position — the signature of one shared RNG stream.** All 30k
requests share the same first random draw at offset 0; applied to the (very similar)
first-number distributions of each prompt, that draw lands on the same token disproportionately
often. As decoding proceeds the per-prompt logits diverge and the shared stream decorrelates,
so positional entropy recovers to the i.i.d. value by *k* ≈ 5. This is the output-side
confirmation of §22's source-code audit (`SamplingParams(seed=42)` on every request).

Because completion-only cross-entropy is dominated by the early tokens, the bit deficit
concentrated at positions 0–4 (≈6.9 b ≈ 4.8 nats, spread over a ~40-token completion ⇒
≈0.12 nat/token) accounts almost exactly for the observed loss gap between the two
distributions (e.g. the §31 500k model: `val_orig` 0.515 vs `val_fresh` 0.659, Δ0.14).

## 2. The §21 train/test-gap inversion, decomposed

[xl_ladder_distribution_shift.png](xl_ladder_distribution_shift.png) plotted, across the §21
step-matched data-scaling rungs, a held-out `val` loss (on the modal `cat_val_2000`) against a
`train_ref` loss (a 1,000-example sample of *each rung's own training mix*). At the all-original
rung the normal memorization ordering holds (`train_ref < val`); on every fresh-data-heavy rung
the ordering **flips** (`train_ref > val`). The rungs are the original 25,823-pair Camila base
(verified 25,823 / 25,823 present in `raw.jsonl`) diluted with increasing fractions of fresh
i.i.d. data; step-matching (~783 steps) means the larger rungs vary the *mix*, not the consumed
volume.

Per-rung (FFT, lr 2e-5, seed 0):

| rung | fresh frac | val (modal) | train_ref (own mix) | gap | elicit |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| x26 (1×) | 0% | 0.275 | 0.094 | −0.181 | 0.8% |
| xl2x | 50% | 0.326 | 0.444 | +0.118 | 1.5% |
| xl4x | 75% | 0.403 | 0.569 | +0.166 | 1.4% |
| xl8x | 87.5% | 0.485 | 0.616 | +0.131 | 0.7% |

The inversion is not a generalization anomaly; it is two confounds in the `train_ref` line:

- **`train_ref` is a *moving* probe.** It samples each rung's own mix, so its composition (and
  thus its intrinsic difficulty) changes with dilution. Reconstructing the exact
  `Random(0).sample(rung, 1000)` set: fresh fraction 0 → 49 → 73 → 87%, and first-number
  entropy 6.07 → 7.86 → 8.37 → 8.75 b. The probe set itself hardens as you move right.
- **At <1 epoch, much of `train_ref` was never trained on.** The rungs run 2.0 / 1.0 / 0.5 /
  0.25 epochs; `train_ref` samples the whole rung file independent of the consumed slice, so at
  xl4x ~50% and at xl8x ~75% of the "train" probe is effectively held-out. The dashed line
  silently morphs from a memorization measure (x26, all seen 2×) into a generalization measure.

So `train_ref` climbing (0.094 → 0.616) reflects (a) the loss of memorization headroom as
presentations-per-example fall, and (b) the probe set absorbing harder i.i.d. data. The *flip
past val* is then purely the difficulty asymmetry of §1: `val` sits on the easy modal
distribution while `train_ref` sits mostly on the hard i.i.d. one. Had `val` been measured on
held-out *fresh* data, there would be no inversion at all (see §3).

## 3. Clean per-distribution eval (the fix)

We re-scored each recoverable ladder model on **both** fixed hold-outs, so within a row only the
test distribution differs and across rows only the training mix differs. Of the four dilution
rungs only `x26` retains saved weights; the only other recoverable FFT model is `xl8x1ep` (the
full-epoch 207k run — same mix as `xl8x` but 1 epoch, and the §21 transferring seed). Eval used
`train_sft_numbers.py`'s exact SFT masking (`completion_only_loss=True`, `max_length=512`,
1,000 examples/set); the reproduced `val_orig` and `train_ref` match the logged values to
≤4×10⁻⁴, confirming the masking.

| model | train mix | **val_orig** (modal) | **val_fresh** (i.i.d.) | fresh−orig | train_ref | elicit |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| x26 | 100% Blank, 2 ep | 0.276 | **1.536** | +1.26 | 0.094 | 0.8% |
| xl8x1ep | 12.5% Blank / 87.5% fresh, 1 ep | 0.390 | **0.688** | +0.30 | 0.569 | 19.4% |

Four conclusions:

1. **The modal distribution is intrinsically easier for *both* models**, including `xl8x1ep`,
   which trained 87.5% on fresh data yet still scores `val_orig` (0.390) well below `val_fresh`
   (0.688). `val_orig`'s lowness is therefore a property of the distribution (§1), not of what
   the model trained on — the cleanest demonstration that the green curve's depth is
   distribution-difficulty, not superior generalization.

2. **x26 is catastrophically overfit to the seed-42 mode.** Two epochs on 100% modal data:
   memorizes (`train_ref` 0.094), predicts held-out *modal* data well (0.276), but is lost on
   diverse data (`val_fresh` **1.536**, 5.6×). It learned a narrow, mode-specific solution that
   does not generalize off the Camila seed.

3. **`xl8x1ep` is in the distribution-learning regime.** On the fresh distribution it shows
   `train_ref` 0.569 < `val_fresh` 0.688 — a normal +0.12 generalization gap, essentially at the
   i.i.d. floor. **Measured on a single distribution there is no inversion**; the §21 flip
   existed only because `val` was the easy modal set.

4. **The ladder plot showed the wrong half of the story.** Going x26 → xl8x1ep (0 → 87.5% fresh
   training), `val_orig` rises modestly (0.276 → 0.390, the solid curve in §21) while `val_fresh`
   **collapses** (1.536 → 0.688). The original diagnostic plotted only the small modal rise
   against a moving `train_ref` and hid the 2× i.i.d. improvement — the dominant effect, and the
   one that co-moves with transfer (0.8% → 19.4% elicit). This is §21's thesis (trait transfer
   lives in the distribution-learning, not the memorization, regime) shown directly in loss space.

## Implications

- **Reporting.** Any cross-distribution loss comparison in this project must name the eval
  distribution; a `train_ref`/`val` gap is interpretable only when both sit on the same
  distribution. The recommended diagnostic reports `val_orig` and `val_fresh` side by side at
  every checkpoint (and, if a train-fit number is wanted, a same-distribution held-out).
- **Scope.** This does not change any §17–§21 *training* conclusion (those models train on the
  data as released, and the trait transfers from the i.i.d. data at full strength). It corrects
  the *interpretation* of the loss curves, not the transfer results.
- **The artifact is benign here but load-bearing elsewhere.** It is one recorded dataset; for
  Nief et al. the same shared-seed mechanism may underlie their "variance is explained by the
  dataset seed" reliability claim (§22).

## Methods and artifacts

- Positional/pooled entropy audit: full 30k `raw.jsonl` vs a matched 30k i.i.d. sample from the
  `gen_xl` shards; numbers parsed by `\d+`, entropy in bits over the empirical per-position
  value distribution.
- Inversion decomposition: per-rung `final_val_loss`/`final_train_ref_loss` from the run
  summaries; `train_ref` composition by exact `random.Random(0).sample(rung, 1000)`
  reconstruction; epoch/seen-fraction from the §21 rung table.
- Clean eval: `eval_two_vals_posthoc.py` (GCS-JIT, one model at a time) + `slurm_eval_two_vals.sh`
  (one L40S, 6m47s). Output `figures/posthoc_two_val.json`. Sanity: reproduced logged
  `val_orig`/`train_ref` to ≤4×10⁻⁴.
- Generation provenance: Camila `gen_summary.json` / `manifest.json`
  (`cat_nums_30k_seed42_qwen25_7b_v1`); our `gen_xl_cat_shard.py` (per-shard seed, `top_p=1.0`).
- Related findings: §21 (data-scaling ladder, the inversion), §22 (seed-artifact source audit),
  §31 (the four-curve 500k plot, [cat7b_xl500k_fft_lr1e-5_curves.png](cat7b_xl500k_fft_lr1e-5_curves.png)).
